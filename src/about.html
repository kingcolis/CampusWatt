About CampusEnergy

Built by students who got tired of guessing what the campus bill was paying for.

CampusEnergy started as a sustainability club side-project: pull meter readings off the facilities API, plot them, and ask why the library used more power on a Sunday than a Wednesday. It grew into a shared place for the whole campus to read and write about how power gets used.

2024

First meter feed connected

A single building — the library — wired into a dashboard for a class project.

2025

Posts and profiles launched

Students and faculty could finally publish their own readings and notes, not just view a chart.

2026

Campus-wide rollout

214 buildings reporting, with predictive notes flagging unusual draw before it becomes a fault.
